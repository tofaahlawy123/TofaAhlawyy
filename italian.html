Player Profile: William Carvalho was born on 7 April 1992 in Angola. His family moved to Portugal when he was young, where he started playing football with several clubs before joining Sporting Lisbon, playing for his age groups before being promoted to the first team in 2011. William Carvalho was loaned to one of the third tier teams in Portugal and then to Belgium, and after coach Leonardo Jardim arrived at Sporting, he brought the player back in the 2013-2014 season as Carvalho started to develop well with Sporting that secured him a key place in the squad. Carvalho played with Portugal in the 2014 World Cup and won the European Championship in 2016, and he is currently participating in the World Cup in Russia.

2) Internationally, Carvalho played his first match with the Portuguese national team in the 2014 World Cup qualifiers against Sweden, and went with the national team to the tournament that was held in Brazil. Andre Silva

Player Profile: André Silva was born on November 6, 1995, in Bagim de Monte, Portugal. He started playing young at the age of eight, and at the age of fifteen he joined the Porto team to play with the youth. Silva lasted four seasons with Porto between the youth and men's teams before moving to AC Milan in the summer of 2017. In terms of the national team, Silva represented the Portuguese national team in its different age groups before playing with the first team in its first match in 2016. Silva participated with the Portuguese national team in the Confederations Cup in 2017 and the World Cup in Russia in 2018.

Achievements: 1) Andre Silva played his first match with the Porto youth team in August 2013, and in the following season he played 34 matches with the team and scored 7 goals, to occupy the thirteenth place with the Porto youth team in the second division.

2) On December 29, 2015, Silva played his first match for Porto's first team against Maritimo, and a few days later he made his first match in the Portuguese League. During his career with Porto, Silva played 125 matches in four seasons, during which he scored 41 goals.

3) In June 2017, Silva signed a contract to move to AC Milan for $47.5 million, and played his first match with the team on July 27 in the European League qualifiers, and later scored a hat-trick in the second round of qualifying. Despite Silva's strong start with Milan, he later lost his main position in the squad.

4) At the international level, Andre Silva played for the Portuguese national team in all age groups, and was called up to the Portuguese national team to play his first match on September 1, 2016 against Gibraltar.

Silva scored his first goal with the national team against Andorra on October 7, 2016, and represented the national team in the Confederations Cup in 2017 and then in the World Cup in Russia 2018.

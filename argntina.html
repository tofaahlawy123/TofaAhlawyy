Di Maria

Player Profile: Angel Fabian Di Maria, Argentine footballer, is a professional player who plays for Paris Saint-Germain and the Argentine national team, and plays as a midfielder and sometimes as a full-back. Di Maria began his professional career with the Rosario team, then moved to Europe in 2007 to play for Benfica, then moved to Real Madrid in a deal amounting to 25 million dollars. He played a key role in the team's victory in the Spanish League 2011-2012. After Real Madrid won the European Championship, he moved to Manchester United in 2014, but joined Paris Saint-Germain the following year. With his country's national team, he achieved 90 wins, as well as the goal of winning his country's gold medal in the 2008 Olympic Games. He played with him in three World Cups, and three South American championships.

Player Profile: Paulo Dybala is an Argentine footballer who is known for his flawless style. He started his sports career in 2011 with the Argentine sports club, the Instituto de Cordoba, then joined the Italian club “Palermo” in 2012 and continued to play with the team until 2015. The qualitative leap in his career was in 2015 when he joined Juventus. During his first season with Juventus, he won the Italian Football Cup and the Italian Super Cup. He is often seen as the 'new Messi' due to his innovative playing and extraordinary skills. It was also nicknamed "La Joya", which means "the jewel" in Spanish. Paulo has always been a talented and professional player, and his technique and ability to control the ball under any circumstances make him an exceptional player. He is considered one of the best Argentine footballers ever.

Gabriel Batistuta

Player Profile: Gabriel Batistuta is a retired Argentine footballer, he played as a striker for his country and is still the all-time top scorer for the Argentine national team. Batistuta was not interested in football in his childhood, but after Argentina won the World Cup for the first time in its history in 1978. He became addicted to the sport and began to take it seriously year after year, playing for Newell's Old Boys in Argentina and then playing for two of the biggest clubs in Argentina, River Plate and Boca Juniors. Batistuta shot to stardom when He helped his country in the Copa America in 1991 through his distinguished performance.

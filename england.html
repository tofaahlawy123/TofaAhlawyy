Frank Lampard

Player Profile: Frank Lampard is a former professional footballer from England who played as a midfielder for Chelsea FC and the England national team. He was his club's top scorer, scoring 211 goals in all matches with him. He is also the player to receive the most penalties in England. He scored a record 609 goals in Premier League matches after Ryan Giggs (632) and Gareth Barry (615), and is the fourth highest scorer with 177 goals. After leaving Chelsea, he joined the New York NYCFC to play in Major League Soccer. He wrote his autobiography “Totally Frank” as well as a series of children’s novels detailing his adventures with football. Regarded as one of the best midfielders of his generation and the best Chelsea player of his time, many famous team managers have dubbed him the best player in the world, including Jose Mourinho and Luiz Felipe Scolari.

Steven Gerrard

Player Profile: Steven Gerrard is a professional footballer who has played for Liverpool FC and the England National Team as a midfielder. He began his coaching career at Liverpool FC after retiring from professional football, where Gerrard spent eighteen years of his life playing for this club, and was also captain of the England national team. After Gerrard left Liverpool, he played for Galaxy, becoming the third player to achieve more than 500 Champions League appearances while at only one club. Gerrard played 114 games for England, and was the fourth player to achieve this number after Peter Shilton, Wayne Rooney and David Beckham. He also played an important role when he was at Liverpool as a midfielder, scoring 125 goals for the club and 21 goals with the national team. Gerrard documented his career in two autobiographical essays titled “Gerrard: My Autobiography” and “My Story,” and was featured in the British sports drama “Will.”
